My advice to Latinos, after 5 years in tech

Published February 4th, 2018

After graduating from Boston College, I joined several tech startups including HubSpot, to jumpstart my career in tech. As I gained experience in working with both the North American and Hispanic markets, I recognize the unique value that Latinos offer in driving business growth, especially, as the US Hispanic consumer market continues to become the world's largest buying power. I know that these insights helped me to find jobs and advance my career, so I decided to take some time to share actionable advice for other Latinos who are starting out in tech.

My advice to new programmers, undergoing a career shift

Published February 16th, 2018

After developing my career in Sales and Marketing, I underwent a career shift to dig deeper into my passion for tech and become more self-sufficient in regards to building my own business and product ideas. In 2017, I took a leap from sales to software engineering and haven't looked back since. My transition was immediately life-changing and more than I had hoped for. Each day, I thank God for enabling me to fuel my relentless pursuit of creativity and innovation through engineering. I am inspired to give back, by planting seed that will grow into fruit, that will be shared with the same world that continues to inspire me. In this post, I share insights to a newfound world of possibilities that will be revealed to you, with your newfound skills.
